The problem: toxic stress

When children don’t have responsive relationships, the toxic stress of poverty can derail early brain development.

Here's why Storytime works

Storytime Promotes Face-to-Face Interaction

Storytime gets families talking face to face with dialogic reading. This starts the back-and-forth interaction that has been shown to reduce stress.

In addition, Storytime has been shown to increase nighttime reading by 160%!



Storytime Builds Phonological Awareness

With Storytime, kids build phonological awareness through visual prompts, which parents can point out to teach rhyme and alliteration.

Phonological awareness is one of the strongest predictors of later literacy.



Storytime Develops Oral Language Skills

Parents use picture prompts to help kids develop oral language skills and back-and-forth talking habits.



Storytime Helps Build Talking Habits

Our texts explain why talking matters and share talking prompts for daily routines. These habits help form stable, responsive relationships.

We’re building relationships so kids can grow.
